Orthogonal vectors

Two vectors are orthogonal if the angle between them is 90 degrees. If two vectors are orthogonal, they form a right triangle whose hypotenuse is the sum of the vectors. The dot product xTy=yTx is zero exactly when x and y are orthogonal. (The length squared ||x||2 equals xTx.) Note: all vectors are orthogonal to the zero vector.

Orthonormal vectors

The vectors q1, q2, ...qn are orthonormal if:
qiTqj=0 if i≠j or qiTqj=1 if i=j.
Note: they have (normal) length 1 and are perpendicular (ortho) to each other. Orthonormal vectors are always independent.

A=QR decomposition

If the columns of Q=[q1...qn] are orthonormal, then QTQ=I is the identity. A square orthonormal matrix Q is called an orthogonal matrix. If Q is square, then QTQ=I tells us that QT=Q−1.
